# Resonant Pitch Scorecard

This scorecard is about the product itself, not how polished the README sounds.

The question is: if you were pitching Resonant as a real product, which actual capabilities strengthen the pitch, which ones are merely nice, and which gaps would make a smart listener push back?

## Current Read

Resonant's strongest pitch is not "AI companion" in the abstract. It is a specific functional claim:

**Resonant turns a model into a persistent, self-directed, locally owned companion system that can remember context, act across channels, and participate in day-to-day life instead of waiting passively for the next chat message.**

That is the thing to score.

That reads as:

- strong product wedge
- real technical substance
- unusually strong ownership story
- some adoption and evidence gaps

## What Actually Makes Resonant Pitchable

- **Continuity is real.** Resonant does not just preserve messages; it structures ongoing identity and context.
- **Autonomy is real.** The agent can initiate routines, watchers, impulses, and failsafe behavior instead of waiting passively.
- **The system can live where users live.** Web, mobile, Discord, Telegram, push, and voice make the companion feel ambient.
- **It is useful, not just emotionally novel.** Command Center gives the product practical gravity.
- **The user owns the stack.** This is a serious differentiator versus hosted companion products.
- **The harness itself is editable.** That makes Resonant feel like both product and platform.

## What Weakens The Pitch At Product Level

- **Setup friction is still high.** The product may be compelling, but the first audience is clearly technical.
- **The strongest benefits are experiential.** That makes them harder to prove quickly without demos, stories, or user evidence.
- **Safety language is less developed than capability language.** In relational products, that imbalance will get noticed.
- **There is not yet a crisp outcome frame.** For example: better retention, more daily engagement, more follow-through on routines, stronger sense of continuity, or higher user trust.

## The Best Function-Led Pitch Angles

If you want to pitch from actual product function, these are the strongest angles I see.

### 1. The "AI That Doesn't Reset" pitch

Resonant is for people who are tired of re-explaining themselves to AI.

The proof points:
- continuity
- memory hooks
- named threads
- context injection
- long-lived companion configuration

### 2. The "AI That Can Reach Back" pitch

Most chat products wait. Resonant can initiate.

The proof points:
- routines
- pulse
- impulses
- watchers
- timers
- failsafe escalation

### 3. The "Own Your Companion" pitch

Most relationship-like AI products are hosted, opaque, and rented. Resonant is local, inspectable, and customizable.

The proof points:
- self-hosted architecture
- local config
- local files
- editable prompts
- skills
- MCP integrations

### 4. The "Companion Plus Daily Utility" pitch

Resonant is not just a character shell. It can participate in the user's real life organization.

The proof points:
- Command Center
- care tracking
- planner
- calendar
- lists
- finances
- stats

## Fastest Ways To Strengthen The Product Pitch

These are not "make the docs prettier" suggestions. They are the fastest ways to make the actual product pitch harder to dismiss.

1. Add one canonical demo flow that proves continuity, autonomy, and utility in one story.

Example shape:
- chat with companion
- companion remembers prior context
- companion creates or updates a routine
- companion follows up later on Discord or web
- user sees continuity rather than reset

2. Define the first success metric.

Examples:
- days of retained use
- percentage of users who keep autonomy enabled
- number of user-initiated versus agent-initiated meaningful interactions
- weekly follow-through on routines or care tasks

3. Make safety and control part of the core pitch, not a side document.

For relational AI, trust is product function.

4. Name the first audience in functional terms.

Examples:
- technical users who want a persistent AI companion they control
- researchers and builders exploring relational agents
- power users who want an AI that can proactively manage routines across channels

## Bottom Line

Resonant is pitchable because the product really does combine several difficult things in one system:

- continuity
- autonomy
- multi-channel presence
- practical utility
- user ownership

That combination is the pitch.

The main thing still missing is not capability. It is proof that these capabilities create a repeatable, must-have experience for a specific first audience.
